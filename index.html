Introduction to MOBA

This page aims to introduce you to the wonderful world of MOBAs

Beginning life as a mod for a game called StarCraft back in 2002, the MOBA genre has taken the world by storm.

Boasting deep customization and high skill ceiling gameplay, MOBAs offer hundreds or thosuands of hours of riveting experiences.

But what exactly is a MOBA? On this page, you will learn all about the mobile online battle arena genre.

MOBA as a definition is slightly nebulous; there are many MOBAs and each has a unique take on the genre.

But there are some key features a MOBA requires to be a MOBA.

The first are characters. Each boasting a unique set of abilities, your character defines your playstyle and role within the game.

Selecting a tough warrior might mean you are supposed to lead the charge, while an unarmored mage should support from afar.

The second aspect of MOBAs are bases, and the defences they contain.

Primarily, your objective is to attack the enemy base where they spawn from.

The game ends when you destroy their base, or they destroy yours.

Along the way, you will encounter some form of a damaging obstacle you must defeat with the help of NPC minions.

Defeating the enemy minions and obstacles rewards resources, which can be spend improving your character for that match.

MOBA Terminologies

reference for the terminologies

Before trying to know what kind of MOBA games are out there and trying to find out how to play MOBA, here are some of the terminologies that you may want to know before playing MOBA.

  • AFK - Not made for MOBAs, but certainly important to know. Stands for Away From Keyboard, meaning they're in the game, but not playing. If you have a person AFK, you generally lose and everyone gets mad.
  • Back or B - To go Back means to return to your spawn point. Generally, this acts as a healing platform and shop, so you'll frequently visit. If someone says they're going b, they're returning back to the spawn.
  • Base - the area where teams begin the game. Protecting this is the top priority, and you only win by destroying the opposing base. Goes by many names, such as Nexus, Core, etc
  • Boss - Another shared concept that has a unique name in each game. Denotes the biggest neutral creature on the map that, when killed, nets a major bonus to your team. In LoL, it's the Baron, while in Dota, it's Roshan.
  • Carry - the hero meant to kill the enemy players. This hero is generally weak early on, requiring time to build up, but will then be able to win the game with just a bit of help
  • Creep - the actual name for the creatures spawned from each base. Killing the enemy's creeps reward you with money and experience. Your Creep Score is the number of creeps you've killed in the game.
  • Creep Wave - a full group of creeps. Each game spawns a set amount of creeps, waits a time, then spawns another group, causing waves of creeps. Hence, creep wave.
  • Crowd Control - Shortened to CC, this denotes attacks or abilities that cause some detriment to the enemy's abilities, be it a movement slow, stun, or a silence.
  • Dive - The act of attempting to kill someone while they're close to a tower. A risky play, as towers tend to hurt, so it takes a full commitment. You'll probably die and everyone will then get mad.
  • Farm - The act of compiling gold through killing creeps. More money means stronger items, which means that the Carry or carries on your team will need a lot of farm.
  • Feed - The act of dying to an enemy player multiple times. If you die a bunch, you are feeding, and the enemy player will get fed. This is to be avoided at all costs. If you're feeding, everyone will get mad.
  • Gank - The act of traveling out of your lane to try and kill the enemy in another lane. Usually called for if the opponent has a stronger hero.
  • Inhibitor or Barracks - The structure at the beginning of each lane that, once destroyed, powers up the enemy creeps.
  • Jungle - Denotes the area filling out the map between lanes. Tends to hold groups of Neutral Monsters that give good amounts of gold and experience, allowing for a player to become the jungler. The jungler farms the neutral monsters and tries to gank all lanes.
  • Lane - The paths that lead from your base to the enemy base. There can be from one to three lanes in a game, generally denoted by where they are on the overall map. Bottom lane runs along the south, Mid lane is in the middle, and Top lane in the north.
  • Last Hit - the act of getting the killing blow on a creature or player. In most MOBAs, only the player that gets the last hit will get the full amount of gold, so learning to Last Hit is an essential skill to succeed.
  • Neutral Monsters - The monsters sprinkled throughout the jungle. They don't fight for any one side, and don't really move out of their spawn point, called a Camp.
  • Push - The act of killing the enemy creeps quickly so that yours can get to the towers and base, allowing you to destroy them. If the enemy's creeps are attacking your stuff, you can say that lane is pushed.
  • River - not in all MOBAs, but the label is given to the center line that runs between the two opposing sides. In LoL and Dota, it's a literal river and is where you find the Boss.
  • Roam - The act of traveling between lanes to gank the opposing players. Usually, a roamer is someone who forsakes any farming to visit lanes as often as possible and hopefully kill the opponents.
  • Skillshot - Abilities that must be aimed manually, rather than just right clicking an enemy. They take practice, and generally separate good players from great ones.
  • Spawn Timer - Neutral monsters spawn at certain intervals in the jungle. For LOL, the spawn timer counts down from when they die, whereas in Dota, they spawn at set times in game. Spawn timers play a big role for the jungler and Bosses.
  • Split Push - the act of attempting to push a lane while your team accomplishes some other goal, causing the enemy to make tough choices.
  • Support - Role given to heroes who generally don't cause much damage, but offer other benefits to the team. If you lose the game, a lot of people will point to the Support, and will subsequently get mad.
  • Tower - structures on the map meant to protect your lane from getting pushed. Towers automatically target and kill enemies in range, starting with the creeps.
These are just some of the terminologies that you will hear from your teammates whenever you are playing MOBA games. There are a lot more terminologies that you will need to learn and the best way for you to learn is to experience playing MOBA by itself.

MOBA Roles

Essential for team composition and strategy, each role has specific responsibilities and plays a different part in achieving victory.

Here's a breakdown of the primary roles you might encounter:

Bot Laner

Also known as... AD Carry, ADC, Carry, DPS

Often filled by heroes that have strong basic attacks whom start out weak in the early game but gradually scale into becoming high value for the team late game by outputting out highly sustained damage.

  • Main responsibilities: Stay safe by remaining close to the Support and behind everyone else in order to deal sustained damage to the enemy opposition.
  • Goals: Focus on farming for items to help supplement damage, be alert of proper positing when engaging in team fights.
  • Example characters: Faceless Void (Dota 2), Jinx (League of Legends), Sharpshooter (Deadlock)

Support

Also known as... Sup

Though the name is self-explanatory, it's more than just that. They help the team with other things such as providing map vision, engaging in crowd control, healing or protecting teammates, amongst various other utilities provided in their toolkit.

  • Main responsibilities: Set up plays, assist in securing objects, and provide consistent support for your teammates.
  • Goals: Focus on assisting Bot Laner as you are considered a duo in the early stages of the game, then provide more utility to the team later on.
  • Example characters: Dazzle (Dota 2), Janna (League of Legends), Barrier Specialist (Deadlock)

Mid Laner

Also known as... Mid, Mage, Assassin, AP Carry

Often as equivalent in high damage output as the Bottom Laner, but through abilities provided in their toolkit rather than standard basic attacks.

  • Main responsibilities: Use abilities to make significant plays or control the map.
  • Goals: Leverage your position in one of the safest parts of the map by bullying the opposing Mid Laner, using your advantage in a centralized position to gain map control and presence throughout.
  • Example characters: Puck (Dota 2), Ahri (League of Legends), Arcane Mage (Deadlock)

Top Laner

Also known as... Top, Tank, Off-Tank, Bruiser, Split Pusher, Skirmisher, Fighter, Top Carry

The main front line against the enemy team, serving as the tank by absorbing most of the opposing team's damage and allowing your own team to retaliate with some of their own. Otherwise, pressuring their own lane by split-pushing.

  • Main responsibilities: Gain experience to have a level advantage and use that advantage to pressure your respective lane.
  • Goals: Stay alive for as long as possible when engaged in team fights so that your Bottom Laner and Mid Laner can both output high opposing damage safely behind you.
  • Example characters: Tidehunter (Dota 2), Garen (League of Legends), Warrior (Deadlock)

Jungler

Also known as... Ganker

Notorious for making other opposing lanes uncertain if they will be invaded unexpectedly. The Jungler traverses mainly between lanes, dictating the appropriate time to initiate an ambush.

  • Main responsibilities: Farm neutral monsters, secure boss monsters and objectives.
  • Goals: Threaten lane oppoents with ganks and sneaky plays.
  • Example characters: Riki (Dota 2), Lee Sin (League of Legends), Striker (Deadlock)

Mobile Legends Bang Bang

reference for the mobile legends information

Mobile Legends: Bang Bang (also known as MLBB) is a mobile multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game developed and published by Chinese developer Moonton, a subsidiary of ByteDance. The game was released in 2016 and grew in popularity, most prominently in Southeast Asia. At its base gameplay, the game puts 2 teams of 5 against each other in real time with at least 20-60 second matchmaking and 10-30 minute matches. Featuring traditional battle arena gameplay, players must fight over three lanes to take the enemy's tower and defend their own while securing objectives in order to pressure the opposing team. Like classic MOBAs, there is no hero training to level up or pay to play angleâ€”winners and losers are decided based on skill, ability, and strategy.

Gameplay:

Mobile Legends: Bang Bang is a multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game designed for mobile phones. The game is free-to-play and is only monetized through in-game purchases like characters and skins. Each player can control a selectable character, called a Hero, with unique abilities and traits. There are six roles that define the main purpose of heroes: Tank, Marksman, Assassin, Fighter, Mage, and Support. These roles determine the responsibilities of players for their respective teams. Players can also set specific builds for heroes which include in-game items and emblems.

Ranked and Classic

Two teams of five players go against each other in real-time. Players will be matched in correspondence with their current ranking. There are seven ranks in the game with Warrior being the lowest followed by Elite, Master, Grandmaster, Epic, Legend, and the highest rank, Mythic. A player can only invite and form a team with other players of similar rank or players that are one rank higher or lower. Taking down the opposing base is the main goal to achieve victory. There are three lanes in the game: The gold lane (top), exp lane (bottom), and the mid lane. Depending on what side of the map (blue or red) a player starts on, the gold and exp lanes will be switching places. Between each lane is the jungle. The jungle can be divided into four parts: two between the enemy's lanes and two between a player's team lanes. The jungle has different creeps and monsters that offer buffs, experience, and gold. A player cannot attack the enemy base directly without taking down opposing turrets in at least one lane first. Each lane has three turrets that shoot at heroes and deal a ton of damage. To attack them without taking damage, a player needs help from their team's minions. Minions constantly spawn at the teams' bases and travel down along each lane. The team to successfully destroy all the turrets in one or all lanes will increase their chances of destroying the opposite team's base. In classic mode, the gameplay is similar. However, winning or losing a match won't affect a player's ranking. Players can also form a whole team regardless of their rank differences and freely choose any heroes along with weekly free ones and any trial card they possess.

League of Legends

League of Legends is a popular online multiplayer game developed by Riot Games. It falls into the genre known as Multiplayer Online Battle Arena (MOBA). In the game, players assume the role of Champions; each with unique abilities and compete in teams of five to achieve objectives like destroying the enemy team's Nexus, which is the core structure located in their base.

The gameplay involves strategic planning, teamwork, and individual skill as players battle across a map called Summoner's Rift. There are different roles and positions, including top lane, jungle, mid lane, and bottom lane; each with specific responsibilities and strategies. League of Legends is renowned for its dynamic and evolving gameplay, with regular updates, new champions, and a vibrant esports scene.

Game Modes:

  1. Summoner's Rift (Ranked or Normal)

    The primary and most popular mode where two teams of five players compete to destroy the enemy's Nexus. The map features three lanes, a jungle, and various objectives like turrets, dragons, and Baron Nashor.

  2. All Randomm All Mid (ARAM)

    A fast-paced, single-lane mode where teams of five battle on the Howling Abyss map. Players are randomly assigned champions and must work together to push down the middle lane and destroy the enemy Nexus.

  3. Ultra Rapid Fire (URF)

    A limited-time mode that dramatically increases the speed of gameplay. Champions have no cooldowns on their abilities, and mana costs are greatly reduced, leading to a highly chaotic and action-packed experience.

  4. Teamfight Tactics (TFT)

    An auto-battler game mode where players build and upgrade a team of champions that automatically fight opponents. The goal is to outlast other players by strategically positioning and equipping champions on a grid-based board.

  5. Co-op vs AI

    A mode where players team up against computer-controlled opponents. It's a great way for beginners to learn the game or for players to practice strategies without the pressure of competing against human opponents.

Deadlock

Deadlock is the newest game from Valve, known for hits like Half Life and Counter Strike.

Not even released yet and on an invite-only basis, it still garners 100k players daily.

What makes this game unique is that it is equal parts MOBA and hero shooter.

Deadlock has made many changes to the MOBA formula to make it steamlined such as:

  • Four lanes instead of three
  • Ziplines down lanes to get you to and from areas quickly
  • A build search feature in the item shop that is community curated
  • Lane rolls apply to all four lanes, letting you move freely to what lane you'd like
  • Stronger creeps to make games move faster
  • No mirror matchups
  • Simplified item descriptions and status effects
  • Advanced movement mechanics such as dash-jumps and wall bounces
  • Item shops near undestroyed friendly towers

Overall, these changes combined with the 3D environemnts create a great experience for new and veterans alike.
